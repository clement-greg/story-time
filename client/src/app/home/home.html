The lighthouse keeper had not spoken to another soul in a very long time, perhaps forty-three days. The sea, however, was never silent — it groaned and whispered against the rocks with the patience of something that has no reason to hurry.

He climbed the iron stairs each morning, wound the mechanism, and watched the beam sweep the fog with mechanical indifference.

Quill suggestions
Precision
Replace vague time reference with "forty-three days" for specificity.
Rhythm
Strong phrase — the cadence echoes the keeper's isolation. Keep it.
Voice
Consider "bureaucratic coldness" — pairs with the mechanical imagery.
